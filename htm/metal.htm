What is metal?

Metal is the phase of declining energy. When we craft metal we put a lot of energy into it, but the energy always dispers as the metal cools and takes on form. It is a sturdy and powerful element. It is also depicted as the fierce tiger which fights the dragon. Maybe it was fate that the countries of the west eventually came to China and like a tiger sunk their teeth into prey and didn't let go.

Metal is born from the earth in which it is found. It produces water which is an odd idea but seen in the water that dropplets can form around a blade. Metal is crafted and shaped by fire so that it can be used like an ax to control the wood.

Metal is perhaps one of the hardest elements to think about in the five element theory. This is because of two different reasons. The first being that most thoughts of elements around the world do not view metal as a distinct element. They might view it as merely earth or the combination of fire and earth. The second reason is that there is a disconnect from the production of metal to the production of water. It is easy to view how most of the elements lead from one to another, except for metal leading to water because it is not often that we wind water droplets condensing on metal.
